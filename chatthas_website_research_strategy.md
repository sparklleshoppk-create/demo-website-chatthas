# 1. BRAND IDENTITY RESEARCH

## 1.1 Brand Story & History

Chattha's was founded by **Waqar Chattha**, a UK-returned professor at a local Islamabad university, who was previously the co-owner of the legendary **Cheema & Chattha's** in F-11, Islamabad. Waqar took the desi breakfast institution he helped build at Cheema & Chattha and elevated it — stripping it of the "hole in the wall" setting and transplanting it into a crisp, almost fine-dining-like environment.

The original branch launched in **Tariq Market, F-10/2 Islamabad**, with a seating capacity of just 40 visitors, but Waqar had a vision: a three-floor setup with rooftop seating to accommodate 200+ guests.

His core philosophy: **"Farm to Table" — the first of its kind in Pakistan.** Chattha's sources its own organic desi ghee and fresh pure milk directly from a farm. Rice for biryani comes from their own farms. Fruits and vegetables are hand-picked from the premium market. Every ingredient passes "rigorous quality tests" before reaching the kitchen.

The brand has since expanded to 4 confirmed branches, with a 5th (Bahria Phase 7) listed as the newest addition.

**DAWN Magazine (2017)** described Chattha's as the restaurant that "rules the roost" in Islamabad's desi food scene — one that "took desi food out of its hole-in-the-wall environs and transplanted it into a crisp, almost fine-dining-like setting."
